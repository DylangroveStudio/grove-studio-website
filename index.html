About

Personal Narrative

As an architect with a passion for mid-century modern principles, I bring a unique perspective to residential design that questions traditional spatial assumptions and creates homes that truly reflect how people live today.

My approach challenges conventional thinking about domestic spaces, drawing on MCM's emphasis on indoor-outdoor connection and functional beauty while adapting these principles for contemporary Australian living.

This perspective allows me to see possibilities others might miss – from reimagining transitions between spaces to creating homes that embrace both privacy and connection. Every design decision is guided by how it enhances your daily experience rather than simply following conventional patterns.

My connection to Melbourne's architecture community spans over a decade, with a deep appreciation for the city's unique blend of historical and contemporary influences. This local knowledge informs my practice and helps create homes that are distinctly Melbourne while embracing universal design principles.

Professional Credentials

With formal training from RMIT University, I've been a registered architect with the Architects Registration Board of Victoria since 2020. My work has been recognised through multiple industry awards, reflecting a commitment to excellence in residential design.

Professional affiliations include the Australian Institute of Architects, which keeps me connected to the latest developments in sustainable design practices and building technologies.

Continuing education in specialised areas like passive design, heritage adaptation, and contemporary materials ensures my approach remains innovative while grounded in practical expertise.

Design Philosophy

My design approach centres on three core principles:

Authentic Modernism - I believe in honouring MCM principles of clean lines, honest materials, and indoor-outdoor connection while adapting them to contemporary Australian contexts.

Livable Sustainability - Environmental consciousness should enhance daily experience, not compromise it. I integrate sustainable solutions that feel natural and elevate comfort.

Personalised Spaces - Your home should reflect your identity and support your lifestyle. My designs begin with understanding how you live and what matters most to you.

Shoreham House

The Brief

With this 1950s coastal home, our clients sought to enhance functionality while honouring the mid-century character they loved. Working within a defined budget, they needed strategic interventions that would transform key spaces without compromising the home's existing charm.

The Outcome

Through thoughtful, light-touch interventions, we created a home that works harder while maintaining its distinctive mid-century appeal. The master bedroom now features custom full-height storage with integrated technology, while the study offers versatile functionality for both work and guests. The laundry was completely reconfigured with dedicated zones for beach gear and pet supplies, complemented by a refreshed bathroom that maintains practical features while introducing modern elements.

The transformation exemplifies how targeted design can dramatically enhance daily living without costly comprehensive renovations.

Black Rock House

The Brief

Our clients purchased this house in 2010, a beachside property with great potential. They needed to adapt the interiors to suit changing uses, as the home suffered from oddly shaped, overly large living and dining areas that were difficult to heat, cool, and furnish effectively. The clients also required a new study with the flexibility to function as an additional bedroom in the future.

The Outcome

By reconfiguring the main living spaces, we created two distinct areas that function far more effectively. The living area now works harmoniously with the television and window positions, connecting better to the outdoors while providing a cozy, intimate atmosphere despite its generous proportions. Our strategic interventions unlocked space for a dedicated study, while custom joinery throughout maintains clean lines characteristic of the home's period.

We've crafted a transitional style that balances contemporary aesthetics with original elements like skirting and cornices. Art lighting and thoughtful details elevate the space without veering into minimalism, creating an environment that feels sophisticated yet deeply liveable.

Bendigo Residence

The Brief

This historically significant miner's cottage required sensitive intervention to preserve its heritage character while creating functionality for contemporary family life. The owners sought to respect the building's past while ensuring it would serve their needs for decades to come.

The Outcome

We created new outdoor connections with a pergola area that provides shade and refuge from the sun and heat—a perfect spot to enjoy a cup of tea or glass of wine with friends while appreciating the beautiful established gardens. Inside, intelligent reconfigurations have made the space flow more naturally and feel more liveable, despite the challenges of working with a very old house featuring multiple extensions built over the years. We've streamlined the layout while preserving all the character and quirks, making small yet impactful interventions that elevate the entire experience.

This project, like all our work, deeply engages with the clients themselves, bringing out elements of the house that speak directly to who they are. We don't create generic spaces; we create specific, special environments that reflect the people who live there. By engaging with the clients' interests and incorporating their collections of art and historic pieces, we've found meaningful ways to showcase Japanese textiles and art throughout the home, creating a residence that truly celebrates its owners' passions.

Richmond Terrace

The Brief

This 2003 architecturally designed terrace house featured a courtyard that wasn't serving the clients effectively, along with some damage to the street-facing areas. Our task was to create a more relaxing, useful space that incorporated a pool within the courtyard while reconsidering how the private space connected to the street. The design needed to balance the benefits of outlook, trees and sunset views with the need for privacy and security in this urban setting.

The Outcome

By reconfiguring the space like a jigsaw puzzle, we managed to incorporate everything the clients desired, though with slightly revised proportions. This thoughtful approach completely unlocked the potential of the area, allowing the clients to swim, barbecue, entertain and relax within a space that feels appropriate to both the location and their aesthetic sensibilities.

The design engages with nature through anthropic intelligent planting, while mirrored glass and black steel create an urban textural quality that acts as a filter and lens to the street. This layered approach maintains the beautiful outlook to the trees, ensures proper ventilation, and captures the afternoon sun, which can be enjoyed from the elevated pool at the rear.

Our smart approach to pool fencing demonstrates how intelligent design can satisfy both regulatory requirements and aesthetic ambitions—by elevating the pool, we've created a backdrop for the greenery and added layers to the courtyard, while the pool's side wall functions as the required safety barrier, eliminating the need for additional fencing and maximising the space from both design and architectural perspectives.

Waikanae House

The Brief

This international project in New Zealand tasked us with rethinking an old house on a prime hilltop site in Waikanae, a coastal town just 45 minutes from Wellington's CBD. The clients sought to maximise the site's amenity and stunning views.

The Outcome

We developed two beautiful beach homes on the site, complete with underground parking, that truly capitalise on the property's unique position—the only hill in Waikanae with views of Kapiti Coast. Our design ensures that coastal vistas are enjoyed from every significant space: the kitchen, living areas, pool, and even the bedrooms upon waking.

The design carefully balances these panoramic views with protection from the western sun through integrated planter boxes, beautiful operable external screening, louvres, and fixed shutters. These elements block afternoon heat while creating lovely patterns of shadow throughout the day, enhancing the experience of the space.

The result is a home with beautiful indoor-outdoor flow and excellent cross-ventilation. We've incorporated passive house technology for sustainability, while using local materials—all stonework and retaining walls are sourced from the local river quarry.

Intergenerational living was a key consideration in the design. The flexible floor plan accommodates various scenarios: a couple in their sixties spending months alone, adult children in their thirties visiting with their own children, holiday gatherings of up to 35 people, or overnight stays for 10-15 guests. The zoning ensures the house never feels too large when minimally occupied, yet provides ample space for everyone to relax during full-capacity visits.

We've also designed for Airbnb potential, incorporating a separate access court with its own courtyard, kitchenette, and living area. This gives the owners flexibility to either occupy the main house and rent the smaller section, or vice versa when travelling overseas—providing supplementary income during retirement.

Middle Park Transformation

The Brief

The client had commissioned this architecturally significant home with another architect. While the design featured wonderful openness, brightness, and sunset views, the western aspect created uncomfortable heat gain that needed addressing without compromising the views.

The Outcome

Our design solution prioritised a carefully positioned pergola that provides essential shade and respite from the heat without obstructing the cherished views. We aligned the pergola with the existing window breakup and structural lines, ensuring it remains visually unobtrusive from inside while maximising the usable outdoor area.

The transformation includes a sliding screen on one side that offers additional privacy from properties below, and we've replaced the original balustrade with an open metal slat structure to maintain sight lines. Planting boxes and groweries soften the edges, creating a cocooned feeling among the treetops where clients can enjoy privacy and control their exposure to the sun.

The ground floor courtyard has been equally transformed, drawing inspiration from Japanese courtyards, onsens, and reflection pools, with touches of South American influence through colour, render, and form. Mid-century modern references appear in accordion panelling, while layers of groweries, night lighting, and gravel on the back wall create a multisensory environment. Our approach engaged deeply with texture, colour, and light, particularly focusing on nighttime illumination that feels inviting, practical, and cozy—transforming a problematic space into one that brings joy to all the senses.

Glenmore Pool House

The Brief

Our clients had a pool in their back garden and an existing shed that they wanted to convert into accommodation for visiting family, particularly the client's father-in-law. The space needed to function both as guest accommodation and as a social area connected to the pool.

The Outcome

We transformed the structure into a versatile space featuring a kitchenette, bedroom, and ensuite bathroom that works perfectly for guests while connecting directly to the pool. Though the father-in-law ultimately decided not to use the space as originally intended, the clients have found unexpected success listing the pool house on Airbnb, where it has maintained 80-90% occupancy for the past four years. This has provided not only additional income but also enriching experiences meeting new people, completely changing the utility of their backyard.

The design uses simple lighting and beautiful textures, with weatherboard cladding that complements the coastal location's style. We created a tasteful, simple design that works within the budget constraints—even incorporating an IKEA kitchen that perfectly suits the space and purpose. This project demonstrates how thoughtful design doesn't always require premium materials to deliver exceptional results.

Sassafras House

The Brief

This beautiful 1930s home in Sassafras featured the compartmentalised rooms typical of its era. The dining room had small windows limiting the garden outlook, while the kitchen was enclosed as a room within a room, creating disconnected living spaces that didn't support contemporary social interaction.

The Outcome

Our approach maintained heritage details while strategically removing portions of walls to create flow between spaces. We preserved the style of the heritage arch across the new opening and extended these period details through the space as if they had always been there. By flipping the kitchen layout and positioning the sink to face the dining room, we've created a space where the host can engage with guests while preparing drinks or meals.

We replaced small windows with full-height doors, transforming the entry experience and social dynamic of the home. Now, when hosting gatherings, the kitchen—where parties naturally begin—offers clear sightlines to arriving guests, creating an intuitive flow that enhances social interaction.

Our approach demonstrates a sophisticated engagement with heritage architecture that goes beyond simply separating old and new elements. Rather than creating stark contrasts, we've developed a transitional style that thoughtfully blends historic and contemporary elements. The sink might appear period-appropriate while the island feels contemporary; traditional wood elements converse with metal overhead cabinets that reference 1930s American aesthetics.

The result is a space that feels timeless and harmoniously integrated—understated at first glance, but revealing its sophisticated design upon closer inspection. Through careful attention to textures, lighting, finishes, and fixtures, we've created a home that respects its heritage while functioning beautifully for contemporary living.

Our Approach

A Transparent Process Built Around Your Vision

Clear communication consistently ranks as clients' most valued quality in an architect. Our structured yet flexible process keeps you informed at every stage while ensuring your vision guides all design decisions. We've developed this approach specifically to make the journey from concept to completion as stress-free and enjoyable as possible.

Stage 1: Discovery Package

Through collaborative exploration, we'll unlock new ways of thinking about your space. Our Discovery Package provides a clear design vision and strategic insights, helping you avoid costly mistakes while adding significant value to your home.

During this phase, we'll:

  • Visit your site to understand your vision and lifestyle needs
  • Explore your property's unique potential
  • Review planning and building requirements
  • Create preliminary sketch concepts
  • Present comprehensive initial designs
  • Discuss budget considerations and next steps

The Discovery Package concludes with a beautifully bound presentation that captures your vision and sets clear parameters for the design process. This fixed-fee service is also an opportunity for us to determine if we're the right fit for your project, with no pressure to make quick decisions about proceeding further.

Stage 2: Project Coordination & Concept Design

We transform initial concepts into reality by bringing together practical considerations with creative vision. This phase bridges the gap between ideas and implementation.

Key inclusions:

  • Getting practical cost insights from builders
  • Refining project scope based on budget feedback
  • Developing schematic floor plans and spatial arrangements
  • Creating initial material and aesthetic direction
  • Producing preliminary 3D visualisations
  • Exploring sustainability considerations and strategies
  • Presenting design options with pros and cons of each approach
  • Refining preferred concept based on your feedback

Stage 3: Design Development

Once the concept is approved, we refine all aspects of the design in greater detail, ensuring everything works together harmoniously.

This phase includes:

  • Detailed floor plans, elevations, and sections
  • Material selections and finishes
  • More sophisticated 3D visualisations
  • Integration of structural, mechanical, and electrical systems
  • Preliminary cost estimates from potential builders

The development phase ensures that the design is both beautiful and buildable, with all systems and details resolved.

Stage 4: Town Planning (if required)

We prepare the application in response to statutory authority requirements, including plans, diagrams, analyses, studies, reports and other information, and formally lodge it as advised.

This phase may include:

  • Preparation of town planning documentation
  • Consultation with relevant authorities
  • Management of the application process
  • Responding to requests for information
  • Guidance through the approval process

Stage 5: Documentation

With the design fully resolved, we prepare comprehensive documentation for building permits, contractor bidding, and construction.

Documentation includes:

  • Detailed construction drawings and specifications
  • Material and finishes schedules
  • Coordination with engineering consultants
  • Building permit application assistance
  • Tender documentation for contractor selection

Clear, thorough documentation is essential for accurate pricing and smooth construction. We take pride in our meticulous attention to detail in this phase.

Stage 6: Construction

Our involvement continues throughout construction to ensure the design is properly executed and to address any challenges that arise.

During delivery, we provide:

  • Regular site visits and construction oversight
  • Resolution of design questions from the builder
  • Review of shop drawings and material samples
  • Assessment of variations, progress claims and extensions of time
  • Monitoring of provisional sums and other monetary items
  • Quality control inspections at key milestones
  • Identification of incomplete work and defects
  • Issuing of progress certificates
  • Determination of practical completion

We remain your advocate throughout construction, working collaboratively with the builder while protecting your interests and maintaining design integrity.
