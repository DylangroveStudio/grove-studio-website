Shoreham House

Project Overview

This beachside residence drew inspiration from mid-century modernist principles while responding to the coastal environment. The client wanted a space that would accommodate their growing family while maximizing views and natural light.

Design Approach

Our design embraced the sloping site by creating a split-level layout that follows the natural contours of the land. The material palette features timber cladding, exposed concrete, and large expanses of glass to frame coastal views.

Key Features

  • Open-plan living areas with seamless indoor-outdoor connection
  • North-facing glazing to maximize passive solar gain
  • Custom timber joinery throughout
  • Integrated sustainable features including rainwater harvesting
  • Carefully positioned clerestory windows for natural ventilation

"We are so glad we trusted his design vision. Our home both looks and feels incredible - the way the light changes throughout the day creates a constantly evolving experience that we never tire of."

— Verity and Mike, Homeowners, Shoreham

Our Approach

At Grove Studio, we believe in a collaborative design process that puts your needs at the center while bringing our expertise to the table. Our approach combines Mid-Century Modern principles with contemporary functionality, creating spaces that are both timeless and practical.

1
Discovery
2
Concept
3
Design
4
Documentation
5
Construction

1. Discovery

We begin by understanding your vision, lifestyle, and requirements through in-depth conversations and site visits. This phase is about building the foundation of our working relationship and defining the project goals.

  • Initial consultation to discuss your aspirations and requirements
  • Comprehensive site analysis and assessment
  • Review of planning and building regulations
  • Preliminary budget discussion

2. Concept

We translate your brief into spatial concepts, exploring various design options that respond to your site and requirements. This phase focuses on the big ideas and overall direction of the project.

  • Development of spatial planning concepts
  • Exploration of form, materiality, and architectural language
  • 3D visualization to help you understand the proposed spaces
  • Feedback sessions to refine the design direction

3. Design

Once the concept is approved, we develop the design in greater detail, resolving spatial relationships, material selections, and technical requirements.

  • Detailed floor plans, elevations, and sections
  • Material and finishes selections
  • Integration of structural and building services
  • Refinement of sustainability strategies

4. Documentation

We prepare comprehensive construction documentation to obtain necessary approvals and enable accurate pricing from builders.

  • Detailed construction drawings
  • Specifications and schedules
  • Coordination with engineering consultants
  • Planning and building permit applications
  • Builder selection assistance

5. Construction

During construction, we act as your advocate, ensuring the design is executed as intended and addressing any issues that arise.

  • Regular site visits to monitor construction quality
  • Review of shop drawings and samples
  • Coordination with builders and consultants
  • Contract administration
  • Practical completion inspection

About Grove Studio

Meet Dylan

Grove Studio was founded by Dylan Robertson, an architect with a passion for creating thoughtful, functional spaces that enhance the way people live.

With over 12 years of experience working in award-winning firms across Australia and internationally, Dylan brings a unique perspective to residential architecture that balances aesthetic vision with practical considerations.

Inspired by the principles of Mid-Century Modern design, Dylan's approach focuses on honest material expression, clarity of form, and a strong connection between indoor and outdoor spaces.

Credentials

  • Master of Architecture, University of Melbourne
  • Bachelor of Design, University of Queensland
  • Registered Architect, Architects Registration Board of Victoria
  • Member, Australian Institute of Architects
  • Certified Passive House Designer

Philosophy

At Grove Studio, we believe that good design enhances daily life. We create spaces that respond to their context, reflect their inhabitants, and stand the test of time both aesthetically and functionally.

We are committed to sustainable design practices that minimize environmental impact while creating healthy, comfortable living environments.

Every project begins with listening and understanding your unique needs, resulting in spaces that truly feel like home.

Sassafras House

Project Overview

This heritage extension project required a delicate balance between preserving the character of the original home while adding contemporary living spaces. The clients, a family of four, wanted to maintain the charm of their 1920s cottage while creating more functional areas for modern living.

Design Approach

Our approach honored the heritage features of the original home while creating a clear delineation between old and new. The extension is characterized by clean lines, generous glazing, and a refined material palette that complements rather than competes with the existing structure.

Key Features

  • Seamless transition between heritage and contemporary elements
  • Double-height void with clerestory windows for abundant natural light
  • Custom joinery that maximizes space efficiency
  • Integrated indoor-outdoor dining area
  • Preserved period details in the original home
