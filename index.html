About

Personal Narrative

As an architect with a passion for mid-century modern principles, I bring a unique perspective to residential design that questions traditional spatial assumptions and creates homes that truly reflect how people live today.

My approach challenges conventional thinking about domestic spaces, drawing on MCM's emphasis on indoor-outdoor connection and functional beauty while adapting these principles for contemporary Australian living.

This perspective allows me to see possibilities others might miss – from reimagining transitions between spaces to creating homes that embrace both privacy and connection. Every design decision is guided by how it enhances your daily experience rather than simply following conventional patterns.

My connection to Melbourne's architecture community spans over a decade, with a deep appreciation for the city's unique blend of historical and contemporary influences. This local knowledge informs my practice and helps create homes that are distinctly Melbourne while embracing universal design principles.

Professional Credentials

With formal training from RMIT University, I've been a registered architect with the Architects Registration Board of Victoria since 2020. My work has been recognised through multiple industry awards, reflecting a commitment to excellence in residential design.

Professional affiliations include the Australian Institute of Architects, which keeps me connected to the latest developments in sustainable design practices and building technologies.

Continuing education in specialised areas like passive design, heritage adaptation, and contemporary materials ensures my approach remains innovative while grounded in practical expertise.

Design Philosophy

My design approach centres on three core principles:

Authentic Modernism - I believe in honouring MCM principles of clean lines, honest materials, and indoor-outdoor connection while adapting them to contemporary Australian contexts.

Livable Sustainability - Environmental consciousness should enhance daily experience, not compromise it. I integrate sustainable solutions that feel natural and elevate comfort.

Personalised Spaces - Your home should reflect your identity and support your lifestyle. My designs begin with understanding how you live and what matters most to you.

Shoreham House

The Brief

With this 1950s coastal home, our clients sought to enhance functionality while honouring the mid-century character they loved. Working within a defined budget of $150,000, they needed strategic interventions that would transform key spaces without compromising the home's existing charm.

The Outcome

Through thoughtful, light-touch interventions, we created a home that works harder while maintaining its distinctive mid-century appeal. The master bedroom now features custom full-height storage with integrated technology, while the study offers versatile functionality for both work and guests. The laundry was completely reconfigured with dedicated zones for beach gear and pet supplies, complemented by a refreshed bathroom that maintains practical features while introducing modern elements.

The transformation exemplifies how targeted design can dramatically enhance daily living without costly comprehensive renovations.

Bendigo Residence

The Brief

The owners of this heritage 1820s miner's cottage sought to preserve its unique character while adapting it to contemporary living standards. They cherished the home's imperfections—its uneven walls and rich textures—but needed enhanced functionality for modern family life.

The Outcome

Our approach celebrated the cottage's heritage while strategically modernising key spaces. The family bathroom was expanded to include a toilet with natural wood finishes and a feature basin showcasing craftsmanship. The kitchen-laundry configuration was reimagined with a sliding barn door, while the living room received a colour palette of deep indigo and earthy tones to enhance the intimate atmosphere.

Throughout the project, we preserved and highlighted the home's charming irregularities while ensuring it now functions perfectly for contemporary living. The result is a seamless blend of historic character and modern convenience.

Richmond Terrace

The Brief

The owners of this Richmond terrace sought to create a unique urban retreat that would blend Balinese-inspired aesthetics with practical inner-city living. Their priorities included enhancing the outdoor entertaining space, adding a swimming pool, updating aesthetic finishes, and resolving persistent drainage and weatherproofing issues.

The Outcome

Working within the constraints of a heritage overlay, we created a hybrid design that balances privacy and openness. By implementing a creative courtyard solution with strategic screening and integrated weather protection, we transformed the outdoor space into a versatile entertaining area that functions year-round.

The Balinese influence comes through in the material selection and spatial flow, creating a tranquil urban oasis despite the compact footprint. Custom detailing including steel plate L-shaped trim and thoughtful weatherproofing solutions have addressed the property's previous technical issues while enhancing its aesthetic appeal.

Waikanae House

The Brief

This coastal new build needed to balance sophisticated Hamptons-inspired aesthetics with practical sustainability features. The clients envisioned a flexible 3-4 bedroom layout that could accommodate multi-generational use, with potential for partial Airbnb rental and dedicated work-from-home space.

The Outcome

We created a contemporary coastal home that seamlessly integrates passive design principles with timeless Hamptons aesthetics. The layout maximises indoor-outdoor connections with generous decking and strategic orientation to capture views while optimising natural light and thermal performance.

The ground floor guest bedroom with separate entrance and kitchenette provides versatile accommodation options, while the first-floor master bedroom with ensuite captures stunning ocean views. Thoughtful future-proofing includes provision for potential lift installation, ensuring the home will adapt to changing needs over time.

Robust materials and meticulous detailing create a low-maintenance yet high-quality finish that will stand the test of time in the coastal environment.

Middle Park Transformation

The Brief

The owners of this architecturally designed seafront home faced challenges with western sun exposure and heat gain. They sought solutions to improve comfort while enhancing the home's connection to its coastal location and complementing their collection of mid-century modern furniture.

The Outcome

Our response centered on introducing batten structures in raw galvanised finishes for both ground and first floors, providing essential shade while complementing the existing architectural style. For the ground floor study, which was particularly affected by heat and glare, we installed a pergola outside the full-height window and created a low-maintenance garden with a calming water feature.

The interior redesign carefully considers the owners' love for colour and existing MCM furniture pieces, avoiding browns and greens in favour of complementary tones that enhance the cosy feel of the space. We also incorporated strategic planning for future mobility needs, ensuring the home will continue to serve its owners for years to come.

Black Rock House

The Brief

The owners of this coastal home sought to upgrade their living spaces to accommodate a dedicated study area and create a more functional living room. This project represented the first stage of a comprehensive transformation, requiring careful consideration of the bigger picture to ensure coherent future development.

The Outcome

Our approach balanced immediate needs with long-term vision, creating spaces that function perfectly now while establishing the groundwork for future phases. Glenmore Pool House

The Brief

Our clients wanted to transform an old shed into a versatile pool house and guest accommodation. They envisioned a space that would enhance their outdoor lifestyle while providing comfortable accommodation for visiting family and friends.

The Outcome

The transformation has completely changed how the family uses their outdoor space. What was once a utilitarian shed is now a sophisticated poolside retreat and fully-equipped guest house. The design maximises connection to the pool area while providing all necessary amenities for comfortable extended stays.

Careful material selection ensures durability against pool chemicals and water exposure, while the aesthetic complements both the main residence and the landscaped outdoor environment. The project demonstrates how reimagining existing structures can add significant value and functionality to a property.

Sassafras House

The Brief

The owners of this Sassafras property loved their home's beautiful green outlook and natural surroundings but struggled with key functional issues: limited connection to the outdoors, an enclosed kitchen with no visual link to the dining area, and restricted outdoor socialising space.

The Outcome

Our design solution created a flexible family space that maintains the home's connection to its lush surroundings while dramatically improving functionality. By reconfiguring the kitchen layout, we established visual connections to the outdoors, increased natural light, and enhanced the social aspect of the space.

A new external access point improves the arrival experience for guests, while the addition of a generous covered outdoor area allows for year-round entertaining regardless of weather conditions. We also flipped the living room layout to maximise enjoyment of the beautiful surroundings while maintaining visual connections throughout the home.

The result is a harmonious balance of indoor comfort and outdoor connection that perfectly suits this family's lifestyle.

Our Approach

A Transparent Process Built Around Your Vision

Clear communication consistently ranks as clients' most valued quality in an architect. Our structured yet flexible process keeps you informed at every stage while ensuring your vision guides all design decisions. We've developed this approach specifically to make the journey from concept to completion as stress-free and enjoyable as possible.

Stage 1: Discovery Package

Through collaborative exploration, we'll unlock new ways of thinking about your space. Our Discovery Package provides a clear design vision and strategic insights, helping you avoid costly mistakes while adding significant value to your home.

During this phase, we'll:

  • Visit your site to understand your vision and lifestyle needs
  • Explore your property's unique potential
  • Review planning and building requirements
  • Create preliminary sketch concepts
  • Present comprehensive initial designs
  • Discuss budget considerations and next steps

The Discovery Package concludes with a comprehensive brief that captures your vision and sets clear parameters for the design process.

Stage 2: Project Coordination & Concept Design

We transform initial concepts into reality by bringing together practical considerations with creative vision. This phase bridges the gap between ideas and implementation.

Key inclusions:

  • Getting practical cost insights from builders
  • Refining project scope based on budget feedback
  • Developing schematic floor plans and spatial arrangements
  • Creating initial material and aesthetic direction
  • Producing preliminary 3D visualisations
  • Exploring sustainability considerations and strategies
  • Presenting design options with pros and cons of each approach
  • Refining preferred concept based on your feedback

Stage 3: Design Development

Once the concept is approved, we refine all aspects of the design in greater detail, ensuring everything works together harmoniously.

This phase includes:

  • Detailed floor plans, elevations, and sections
  • Material selections and finishes
  • More sophisticated 3D visualisations
  • Integration of structural, mechanical, and electrical systems
  • Preliminary cost estimates from potential builders

The development phase ensures that the design is both beautiful and buildable, with all systems and details resolved.

Stage 4: Town Planning (if required)

We prepare the application in response to statutory authority requirements, including plans, diagrams, analyses, studies, reports and other information, and formally lodge it as advised.

This phase may include:

  • Preparation of town planning documentation
  • Consultation with relevant authorities
  • Management of the application process
  • Responding to requests for information
  • Guidance through the approval process

Stage 5: Documentation

With the design fully resolved, we prepare comprehensive documentation for building permits, contractor bidding, and construction.

Documentation includes:

  • Detailed construction drawings and specifications
  • Material and finishes schedules
  • Coordination with engineering consultants
  • Building permit application assistance
  • Tender documentation for contractor selection

Clear, thorough documentation is essential for accurate pricing and smooth construction. We take pride in our meticulous attention to detail in this phase.

Stage 6: Construction

Our involvement continues throughout construction to ensure the design is properly executed and to address any challenges that arise.

During delivery, we provide:

  • Regular site visits and construction oversight
  • Resolution of design questions from the builder
  • Review of shop drawings and material samples
  • Assessment of variations, progress claims and extensions of time
  • Monitoring of provisional sums and other monetary items
  • Quality control inspections at key milestones
  • Identification of incomplete work and defects
  • Issuing of progress certificates
  • Determination of practical completion

We remain your advocate throughout construction, working collaboratively with the builder while protecting your interests and maintaining design integrity.
